"Dear Emma bears every thing so well," said her father.Emma must do Harriet good: and by supplying her with a new object of interest, Harriet may be said to do Emma good.The speech was more to Emma than to Harriet, which Emma could understand.Emma thought with pleasure of some change of subject.Mrs. Weston and Emma were sitting together on a sofa.Of the lady, individually, Emma thought very little.Emma saw how Mr. Weston understood these joyous prospects.CHAPTER III   This little explanation with Mr. Knightley gave Emma considerable pleasure.Emma was quite eager to see this superior treasure.Emma and Harriet went together; Miss Bates and her niece, with the Eltons; the gentlemen on horseback.The Eltons walked together; Mr. Knightley took charge of Miss Bates and Jane; and Emma and Harriet belonged to Frank Churchill."Mr. Frank Churchill and Miss Woodhouse flirted together excessively."-- Emma had promised; but still Harriet must be excepted.Emma looked at her, quite unable to speak.Knightley and Harriet Smith!--Such an elevation on her side!Emma smiled, and felt that Mr. Weston had very good reason for saying so.Emma could not bear to give him pain.
